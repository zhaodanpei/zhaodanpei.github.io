Biography

She received the Ph.D. degree in optical engineering from the Changchun Institute of Optics, Fine Mechanics and Physics, Chinese Academy of Sciences, in 2006. From 2006 to 2008, she was at Beihang University for postdoctoral research. From 2014 to 2015, she was working at the Department of Computer Science, Rutgers, The State University of New Jersey, USA, as a Visiting Scholar. She is currently an Associate Professor and a Ph.D. Supervisor at the Department of Aerospace Information Engineering, Beihang University. Her research interests include saliency detection, target detection and recognition, image understanding, and their application in remote sensing images. She serves as a Standing Member for the Executive Council of Beijing Society of Image and Graphics. She founded and developed AIRVIC lab.

She received fundation from the National Natural Science Foundation of China, the “十一五” and “十二五” Pre-research Project, the National 863 Project, the National 973 sub-project, the Air Force Pre-research Project, the National Key Research and Development Project, etc. We have also developed a number of research projects on automatic object detection and recognition of remote sensing images cooperated with space institutes and enterprises. Our achievements have been successfully applied to the lunar rover Yutu, high-resolution earth observation and military application.

Recruitment

AIRVIC Laboratory Abstract: Since 2008, Dr. Zhao has been dedicated to exploring key techniques for detecting and recognizing typical targets in complex backgrounds. Her research mainly encompasses machine intelligence and remote sensing, including remote sensing image processing and interpretation, space object identification, as well as AI edge computing and deployment. The specific research directions of the laboratory are as follows:

  1. Automatic detection and recognition of objects in high-resolution remote sensing images.
  2. Investigation of visual salience theory and its application in remote sensing.
  3. Advancement of space-based image information processing technologies.
  4. Development and utilization of high-speed embedded systems for real-time image processing in space-based applications.

We accept applications for postdoctoral, full-time Ph.D., and master’s positions throughout the year. Please submit your resume to my email address. Before reaching out to us, we kindly request that you take some time to review the papers published by our laboratory in recent years. This will not only help you gain an understanding of our research focus but also enable you to excel during any potential interviews that may follow.
