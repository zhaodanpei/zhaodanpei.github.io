Biography

I am an Associate Professor and a Ph.D. Supervisor at the Department of Aerospace Information Engineering, Beihang University.

I obtained the Ph.D. degree in optical engineering from Changchun Institute of Optics, Fine Mechanics and Physics, CAS, in 2006. From 2006 to 2008, I was at Beihang University for postdoctoral research. From 2014 to 2015, I was working at the Department of Computer Science, Rutgers, The State University of New Jersey, USA, as a Visiting Scholar. I lead the AIRVIC Lab.

Research Area

  • Machine intelligence
  • Remote sensing image interpretation
  • Lightweight DL model
  • Emboddied AI

Recruitment

AIRVIC Laboratory has focused since 2008 on key techniques for detecting and recognizing typical targets in complex backgrounds, spanning pattern recognition, machine intelligence, computer vision, remote sensing image processing, space object detection, and model compression.

The specific research directions of the laboratory are as follows:

  1. Automatic detection and recognition of objects in remote sensing images.
  2. Advancement of space-based image information processing technologies.
  3. High-speed embedded systems for real-time image processing.

We accept applications for postdoctoral, full-time Ph.D., and master’s positions throughout the year. Please submit your resume to my email address. Before reaching out to us, we kindly request that you take some time to review the papers published by our laboratory in recent years. This will not only help you gain an understanding of our research focus but also enable you to excel during any potential interviews that may follow.
